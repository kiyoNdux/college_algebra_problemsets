Substitution Method
Problem 1
Solve the system of equations:
x + 2y = 7
3x - y = 8
Solution:
  1. From the first equation, express x in terms of y:
    x = 7 - 2y
  2. Substitute this expression into the second equation:
    3(7 - 2y) - y = 8
  3. Simplify:
    21 - 6y - y = 8
    21 - 7y = 8
    -7y = 8 - 21
    -7y = -13
    y = 13/7
  4. Substitute back to find x:
    x = 7 - 2(13/7)
    x = 7 - 26/7
    x = 49/7 - 26/7
    x = 23/7
  5. Therefore, the solution is x = 23/7 and y = 13/7.
Problem 2
Solve the system of equations:
2x - 3y = 1
4x + y = 14
Solution:
  1. From the second equation, express y in terms of x:
    4x + y = 14 â‡’ y = 14 - 4x
  2. Substitute this into the first equation:
    2x - 3(14 - 4x) = 1
  3. Simplify:
    2x - 42 + 12x = 1
    14x - 42 = 1
    14x = 43
    x = 43/14
  4. Substitute back to find y:
    y = 14 - 4(43/14)
    y = 14 - (172/14)
    y = 196/14 - 172/14
    y = 24/14 = 12/7
  5. Therefore, the solution is x = 43/14 and y = 12/7.
Elimination Method
Problem 1
Solve the system of equations:
3x + 2y = 7
5x - 2y = 3
Solution:
  1. Add the two equations to eliminate y:
    (3x + 2y) + (5x - 2y) = 7 + 3
    8x = 10 â‡’ x = 10/8 = 5/4
  2. Substitute x = 5/4 into the first equation:
    3(5/4) + 2y = 7
    15/4 + 2y = 7
    2y = 7 - 15/4 = 28/4 - 15/4 = 13/4
    y = 13/8
  3. Therefore, the solution is x = 5/4 and y = 13/8.
Problem 2
Solve the system of equations:
4x + 3y = 10
2x - 3y = 8
Solution:
  1. Add the two equations to eliminate y:
    (4x + 3y) + (2x - 3y) = 10 + 8
    6x = 18 â‡’ x = 3
  2. Substitute x = 3 into the first equation:
    4(3) + 3y = 10
    12 + 3y = 10
    3y = 10 - 12 = -2
    y = -2/3
  3. Therefore, the solution is x = 3 and y = -2/3.
Graphing Method
Problem 1
Use the graphing method to solve the system of equations:
y = 2x - 1
y = -x + 5
Solution:
  1. Set the two equations equal to each other:
    2x - 1 = -x + 5
  2. Solve for x:
    2x + x = 5 + 1
    3x = 6 â‡’ x = 2
  3. Substitute x = 2 into either equation to find y:
    y = 2(2) - 1 = 4 - 1 = 3
  4. Therefore, the solution is (2, 3).
Problem 2
Use the graphing method to solve the system of equations:
x + y = 6
2x - y = 3
Solution:
  1. From the first equation, y = 6 - x.
  2. Substitute into the second equation:
    2x - (6 - x) = 3
    2x - 6 + x = 3
    3x = 9 â‡’ x = 3
  3. Substitute x = 3 into y = 6 - x:
    y = 6 - 3 = 3
  4. Therefore, the solution is (3, 3).
